Finetuning Vision-Language-Action Models Requires Fewer Layers Than You Think

Despite their remarkable capabilities, Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models impose prohibitive computational costs at fine-tuning and deployment time. We reveal a highly non-trivial architectural property of leading continuous-control foundations (π0, GR00T-N1.5): despite training on diverse physical trajectories, these models exhibit severe layer-wise representational redundancy.

To exploit this, we introduce CLP — a training-free structural compression pipeline. Using a single forward pass via Centered Kernel Alignment (CKA), CLP identifies and removes redundant twin layers, permanently compressing model depth by up to 50% across both the VLM backbone and the continuous-control policy head, without any auxiliary routing or early-exit modules.

Downstream fine-tuning of the streamlined architecture delivers a dual benefit: 40–50% faster training and up to 30% faster real-time inference, while matching or exceeding full-scale performance. We validate across three simulation benchmarks (LIBERO, RoboCasa, SimplerEnv) and 10 real-world manipulation tasks spanning 4 robotic embodiments — proving that advanced VLAs require significantly fewer layers than previously assumed.

Contributions

  • Accessible Adaptation of SOTA VLAs: We demonstrate that SOTA continuous-control foundations (such as π0 and GR00T-N1.5) can be fine-tuned with substantially reduced depth, lowering memory, training, and inference cost without introducing auxiliary routing or early-exit modules.
  • Pre-Finetuning Backbone Compression: To this end, we propose CLP, a simple calibration-based framework that identifies representationally redundant transformer blocks using CKA and removes them before downstream adaptation.
  • Diversified Multi-Embodiment Validation: We conduct extensive evaluations across multiple simulation benchmarks and 10 real-world manipulation tasks spanning four robotic embodiments, demonstrating that our non-invasive depth reduction is robust, platform-agnostic, and highly generalizable. Beyond achieving competitive performance against prior acceleration methods, CLP consistently improves sample efficiency under limited-data regimes, acting as an effective structural regularizer.

Key Findings

VLA layers are highly redundant

CKA similarity heatmaps reveal that large contiguous blocks of layers in both π0 and GR00T-N1.5 produce nearly identical hidden representations — dark red regions indicate near-perfect similarity. This redundancy exists across the VLM backbone, action expert, VL self-attention, and DiT heads, motivating our training-free compression approach.

Up to 2/3 of transformer layers can be pruned with minimal performance loss

Layer pruning ratio vs. success rate on LIBERO (π0) and RoboCasa (GR00T-N1.5). Both models maintain flat performance profiles up to a 50% pruning ratio, enabling FLOPs reductions of ×1.39 and ×1.42 respectively with negligible loss in success rate, validating that a substantial fraction of deep VLA layers are functionally redundant.

Trains much faster, even performs better

CLP-compressed models finish fine-tuning significantly faster than the full-scale baseline — while matching or exceeding task success rates. On BananaToPot, CLP reaches 75% success in far less time than the base model achieving 65%.

Pruned model recovers base model behavior

Hidden state PCA shows that before fine-tuning, the pruned model's representations diverge from the base model. After fine-tuning, the distributions realign closely — confirming that CLP preserves the representational structure of the original VLA despite removing up to half its layers.

Method

CKA-Guided Layer Pruning (CLP)

  • CKA profiling: Sample batches from diverse datasets and feed them through the pretrained VLA, saving hidden states from every layer to compute pairwise CKA similarity scores.
  • Block identification: Using a threshold τ, group consecutive layers into redundant blocks where every adjacent pair has CKA similarity ≥ τ. Within each block, keep only the first layer and prune all remaining ones.
  • Training-free compression: No auxiliary modules, no routing — the pruned architecture is obtained from a single forward pass. Removes 33–66% of transformer layers with ~30% FLOP reduction.
  • Downstream fine-tuning: The streamlined model is fine-tuned on the target task, cutting training time by up to 50% and inference time by 30–50%.
  • Behavior preserved: Hidden state PCA confirms the fine-tuned pruned model recovers the internal representational structure of the original base model.
